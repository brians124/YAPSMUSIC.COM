Biography

Nova Eclipse was born in Lagos, Nigeria, and raised on a diet of Fela Kuti vinyl and her grandmother's church choir. From her earliest memories, music was not simply a passion — it was a language, one she spoke before she could read. By the time she was sixteen, she was performing at local festivals, weaving Yoruba lyricism into driving electronic beats that defied easy categorization.

Her debut album Electric Harmattan (2019) announced a bold new voice in Afrobeats fusion. The album's lead single, "Fire Tonight," became an anthem across West Africa within weeks of release, eventually charting in over twenty countries. Critics praised her ability to root deeply personal narratives in universally accessible grooves — a rare gift that only a handful of artists in a generation possess.

By 2022, Nova Eclipse had collaborated with names like Burna Boy, WizKid, and UK producer MIST — all while insisting on creative control of every element of her artistry. Her sound evolved with each release, drawing from dancehall, R&B, and neo-soul while never abandoning the Afrobeats core that made her name. The Cosmic Frequency album cycle cemented her as a headliner, selling out arenas from Accra to Amsterdam.
